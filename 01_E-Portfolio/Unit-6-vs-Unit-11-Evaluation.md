# Evaluation of Unit 11 Final Project Against Unit 6 Status Document
## Introduction
This section evaluates how the final Individual Executive Summary (Unit 11) developed from the earlier Team Status Document produced in Unit 6. It highlights the progression of the project, identifies the improvements made throughout the module, and reflects on how the application of security and risk management principles became more comprehensive and evidence-based.

## Comparison of Project Scope
The Unit 6 Status Document focused primarily on identifying the organisation's current security posture, existing risks and the initial impact of digitalisation. It established a baseline understanding of the organisation and divided the work between team members to assess different areas of risk. In contrast, the Unit 11 Individual Executive Summary expanded this foundation into a comprehensive strategic risk assessment by evaluating probabilities, analysing uncertainty through quantitative risk modelling, and recommending evidence-based mitigation strategies supported by recognised security and risk management frameworks.

## Development of My Individual Contribution
My contribution also developed significantly between the two projects. During Unit 6, I was responsible for analysing the risks associated with the organisation's digitalisation and collaborating with my teammate to produce the overall status document. By Unit 11, I independently completed the entire executive summary, requiring me to identify risks, justify the selection of quantitative risk assessment methods, develop a Monte Carlo simulation, evaluate business continuity strategies and produce evidence-based recommendations. This progression demonstrates how my technical knowledge, analytical ability and confidence developed throughout the module.

## Evaluation of Project Outcomes
Comparing the two projects demonstrates clear progression in both the quality of the analysis and the maturity of my decision-making. The Unit 6 Status Document established the project's direction by identifying risks and proposing initial recommendations. The Unit 11 Executive Summary built upon this foundation by providing deeper analysis, stronger justification of decisions, quantitative modelling and strategic recommendations supported by academic literature. This progression reflects both the cumulative learning achieved throughout the module and my increased ability to apply security and risk management principles to realistic organisational scenarios. Overall, the comparison demonstrates a clear progression from an initial collaborative assessment to an independently developed, evidence-based executive summary, reflecting both the objectives of the module and my professional development as a cybersecurity practitioner.
