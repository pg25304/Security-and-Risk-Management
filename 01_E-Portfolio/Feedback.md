# Feedback and Actions Taken

## Feedback from Peers and Tutors

## Peer Feedback

### Collaborative Discussion 1

During Collaborative Discussion 1, I received constructive feedback from Moza and Séba regarding the ethical risks associated with using artificial intelligence and digital technologies in human rights investigations.

Moza emphasised the importance of conducting Human Rights Impact Assessments (HRIAs) before implementing technological systems. This feedback highlighted the need to identify potential discrimination, surveillance risks, and harms to vulnerable communities before deployment. Moza also reinforced the importance of explainable AI, transparency mechanisms, and audit trails.

Séba extended the discussion by highlighting the dangers of investigators becoming overly reliant on algorithmic outputs. His contribution emphasised that accountability depends not only on technological systems themselves but also on investigators retaining the ability to critically assess and verify automated findings.

### Collaborative Discussion 2

During Collaborative Discussion 2, I received valuable feedback regarding the limitations of the Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS).

Moza suggested that organisations should avoid using CVSS as a standalone prioritisation mechanism and instead combine it with additional contextual factors such as asset criticality, exploit availability, and business impact. She also proposed developing internal scoring guidelines and calibration sessions to improve consistency among analysts.

Séba highlighted the distinction between likelihood and impact. He argued that although EPSS addresses some limitations of CVSS, it does not fully assess organisational consequences. This feedback helped me understand that neither CVSS nor EPSS should be used independently and that vulnerability prioritisation requires a multidimensional approach.

## Actions Taken

The feedback received from my peers broadened my approach to cybersecurity risk assessment by encouraging me to consider technical, organisational, legal and ethical perspectives together. Rather than relying on a single viewpoint, I learned to evaluate security decisions within their wider business context, helping me make more balanced and evidence-based judgements.

This experience also reminded me not to become too confident in my own assumptions. Listening to different viewpoints encouraged me to question my initial opinions and consider alternative approaches before reaching a conclusion. I believe this has made me a more reflective and open-minded cybersecurity practitioner.

## Tutor Feedback

The tutor commended my ability to analyse the case study, apply appropriate risk assessment standards and frameworks, and consider wider organisational and business factors when evaluating security risks. The feedback also recognised the quality of my critical analysis and the clear structure and presentation of the assignment.

This feedback increased my confidence in applying structured risk assessment approaches while reminding me that selecting an appropriate method is only part of the process. Equally important is critically evaluating its strengths, limitations and suitability for the situation.

The tutor also encouraged me to strengthen my critical evaluation by discussing the limitations, assumptions and practical challenges associated with applying different risk assessment methodologies. This highlighted the importance of not only selecting an appropriate approach but also demonstrating an awareness of its weaknesses and the contexts in which alternative methods may be more suitable.

### Actions Taken

The tutor's feedback had a direct influence on my approach to the Individual Executive Summary. Rather than focusing only on selecting an appropriate risk assessment method, I made a conscious effort to evaluate the strengths, limitations and assumptions of each approach before justifying my final choice. I compared Monte Carlo simulation with alternative methods, including Bayesian analysis and MCDA, before explaining why it was the most appropriate technique for the Pampered Pets scenario.

Looking back, I can clearly see how this feedback improved the quality of my work. It encouraged me to justify my decisions more critically rather than simply explaining them, resulting in a more balanced and evidence-based executive summary.
